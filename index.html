About

I’m a Game Developer with a heavy focus on programming, though I’ve always had a soft spot for the artistic side of things. I started coding at 15 just out of curiosity, messing around with HTML and CSS. That eventually evolved into scripting for FiveM with Lua, and later, making full games in Godot and Unity. Right now I’m tackling Unreal Engine and learning both Blueprints and C++.

Programming is definitely my main thing since I love building mechanics and optimizing code, but I’m not just about the logic. I’ve loved graffiti since I was a kid and I’m also super into fashion. I put a lot of thought into curating my own distinct style because I think having that visual sensitivity actually helps build a better personality.

I’m 20 and currently in my third year of the VAM course at Universidade Lusófona. It’s honestly been a huge challenge balancing my degree with my job at a refurbished tech company. I’m the sole person responsible for managing RMAs there which basically means I spend a lot of time fixing computers and diagnosing hardware issues. It’s crazy how much that mirrors coding since trying to track down a hardware fault uses the exact same logic as debugging a game. I even write mini scripts sometimes to optimize our processes there. It really trains my brain to hunt for errors and find creative solutions. The pressure is real though. I can't drop the ball at work because the department depends on me, but I can't let my grades slip either.

At the end of the day, I just want to build games that feel good to play and actually leave a mark on the player.
